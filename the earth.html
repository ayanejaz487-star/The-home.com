THE EARTH

➢ The Core

The Earth’s core is the very center of our planet. It is a big, hot ball of iron and nickel. The core consists of two layers: the inner core and the outer core. The inner core is solid because of the massive pressure from the layers above it. It’s as hot as the surface of the sun! The outer core is liquid and flows around the inner core, creating Earth’s magnetic field, which protects us from space radiation.

➢ The Mantle

The mantle is the thickest layer of the Earth, located between the core and the crust. It is made of hot, semi-solid rock that behaves like very thick syrup. This rock moves slowly in cycles called convection currents, which are responsible for moving the continents and causing earthquakes. The mantle is about 1,800 miles (2,900 km) thick and makes up nearly 80 percent of the Earth’s total volume.

➢ The Crust

The crust is the most exposed layer of the earth. It is the earth’s hard outer shell, the surface on which we are living. In relation to the other layers, the crust is much thinner. It floats upon the softer, denser mantle. There is an Oceanic crust (thinner, made of basalt) and a Continental crust (thicker, made of granite).

➢ The Atmosphere

The earth is surrounded by various gases. It consists of 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, and 1% other gases. The Troposphere is where weather happens, and the Stratosphere contains the ozone layer that protects us from UV rays. The atmosphere is about 500 miles (800 km) thick.
